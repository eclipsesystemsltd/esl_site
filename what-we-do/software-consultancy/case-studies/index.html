Case Studies

Our experienced consultants specialise in solving hard technical and business problems.

Take a look at the case studies below for some examples of our consultancy work, or give us a call to find out how we can help you.

FCO

More

During my time at the FCO I worked on two main projects. The first of these was an intelligent, high volume, multi-threaded, real-time, secure network communication system. The system essentially comprised of a multi-tiered Java J2EE management application that controlled several Java subsystems that were effectively context sensitive message routers/processors. The management application was built around the use of Spring, Hibernate and MySQL, and was deployed to a JBOSS application server with JMS queues being used to communicate with the subsystems. Distributed transactions were used between the MySQL database and the JMS queues and these were configured using Spring AOP and XA data sources within JBOSS. The management application was also provided with a SOAP API created using Spring web services, this API enabled a remote JSF/IceFaces based web application to connect to and control the system. Because the system was highly secure, authentication, authorisation and key management processes were important, as was XML manipulation using JAXB to cover varying message formats. I worked on most aspects of this project, particularly in the Spring, Hibernate, JMS, web service and security areas.

The second project I worked on was similar to the first and my particular role was to transfer a number of technologies that I had used on the first project to the second, specifically Spring, JPA/Hibernate and Spring web services. This second project was Glassfish and MySQL based. On this project I was also more involved with the JSF/IceFaces web interface and additionally implemented several BIRT reports embedded into the web interface. Software was developed using Eclipse or Netbeans and built with Ant or Maven. Code was tested using JUnit, JMock and DBUnit where needed. In addition to coding at various times I was involved in testing, deployment, documentation and program maintenance.

The Foreign and Commonwealth Office (FCO), commonly called the Foreign Office, is a department of the United Kingdom government. It is responsible for protecting and promoting UK interests worldwide. It was created in 1968 by merging the Foreign Office and the Commonwealth Office.

The head of the FCO is the Secretary of State for Foreign and Commonwealth Affairs, commonly abbreviated to "Foreign Secretary" (currently William Hague). This position is regarded as one of the four most prestigious appointments in the Cabinet, alongside those of Chancellor of the Exchequer and Home Secretary. Together with the Prime Minister, these are the Great Offices of State.

The FCO is managed from day-to-day by a civil servant, the Permanent Under-Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs, who also acts as the Head of the Diplomatic Service. This position has been held by Simon Fraser since late August 2010.

Sony BPRL

More

This project was a media asset management system involving digital video analysis. The server was written in Java and its architecture was based on Spring & Hibernate incorporating a MySQL database & exposing functionality via an Axis2 WSDL web service. There were two clients, one being Swing based & the other being a web application using Servlets/JSPs, both accessing the server via the web service. The core image processing was carried out by a C++ executable using the COM based DirectShow & Apple Quicktime. Java development was carried out in Eclipse. C++ development was done in VisualStudio with CPPUnit & MockPP for testing. The project used agile management, was test-driven with a large degree of pair programming.

Sony Broadcast & Professional Research Laboratories (also known as BPRL), is a subsidiary of Sony, focused primarily on their line of professional Business-to-business software. BPRL is located at the Sony Professional facility in Basingstoke, United Kingdom.

Sony Corporation, commonly referred to as Sony, is a Japanese multinational conglomerate corporation headquartered in Kōnan Minato, Tokyo, Japan. Its diversified business is primarily focused on the electronics, game, entertainment and financial services sectors. The company is one of the leading manufacturers of electronic products for the consumer and professional markets. Sony is ranked 87th on the 2012 list of Fortune Global 500.

Sony Corporation is the electronics business unit and the parent company of the Sony Group, which is engaged in business through its four operating segments – Electronics (including video games, network services and medical business), Motion pictures, Music and Financial Services. These make Sony one of the most comprehensive entertainment companies in the world. Sony's principal business operations include Sony Corporation (Sony Electronics in the U.S.), Sony Pictures Entertainment, Sony Computer Entertainment, Sony Music Entertainment, Sony Mobile Communications (formerly Sony Ericsson), and Sony Financial. Sony is among the Worldwide Top 20 Semiconductor Sales Leaders and third-largest television manufacturer in the world, after Samsung Electronics and LG Electronics.

The Sony Group is a Japan-based corporate group primarily focused on the Electronics (such as AV/IT products and components), Game (such as PlayStation), Entertainment (such as motion pictures and music), and Financial Services (such as insurance and banking) sectors. The group consists of Sony Corporation (holding and electronics), Sony Computer Entertainment (games), Sony Pictures Entertainment (motion pictures), Sony Music Entertainment (music), Sony/ATV Music Publishing (music publishing), Sony Financial Holdings (financial services) and others.

Its founders Akio Morita and Masaru Ibuka derived the name from sonus, the Latin word for sound, and also from the English slang word "sonny", since they considered themselves to be "sonny boys", a loan word into Japanese which in the early 1950s connoted smart and presentable young men. The company's current slogan is BE MOVED. Their former slogan was make.believe (2009–2014) and like.no.other (2005–2009).
